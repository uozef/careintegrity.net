Executive Context
The NDIS: A National Success — and a Growing Complexity Challenge
Rapid growth has outpaced real-time oversight capability, creating a widening gap between program scale and integrity infrastructure.

Rising Plan Complexity

Participant plans are increasingly complex with multiple providers, service types, and funding categories.

Fragmented Provider Ecosystem

270,000 providers create inconsistent billing behaviours that are impossible to monitor manually.

Variable Billing Behaviours

Without standardised oversight, billing anomalies go undetected until significant financial leakage occurs.

Reactive Auditing

Current tools identify problems months after claims are processed — by then, the damage is done.

The critical gap: No real-time understanding of system-wide behaviour. The NDIS operates without continuous visibility into provider networks, billing trends, or emerging systemic risk.

Core Insight
Integrity Risk Is Systemic, Not Transactional

Most integrity issues don't emerge from single suspicious claims — they emerge from patterns that only become visible when you view the system as a connected network.

Provider Behaviour Over Time

Gradual drift in billing patterns signals emerging risk long before individual invoices look wrong.

Network Relationships

Clusters of connected providers sharing staff, locations, and participants reveal coordinated abuse.

Care Feasibility

Claimed services that are physically impossible given time, staffing, and geography — workers billing at two locations simultaneously, 24-hour days, impossible travel.
